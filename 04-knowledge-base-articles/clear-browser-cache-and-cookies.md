# How to Clear Your Browser Cache and Cookies

**Applies to:** Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, Microsoft Edge, Safari  
**Last reviewed:** April 2026  

---

## What is a browser cache?

When you visit a website, your browser saves copies of images, scripts, and other page elements in a local storage area called the **cache**. This speeds up future visits to the same site because the browser can load saved files instead of downloading them again.

**Cookies** are small text files that websites store on your device to remember information such as your login status, language preferences, and items in a shopping cart.

Over time, outdated cache files and cookies can cause problems such as pages not loading correctly, login issues, or outdated content displaying instead of the current version.

---

## When to clear your cache and cookies

Clear your cache and cookies when you experience any of the following:

- A website is not loading or displays an error
- A page looks different on your device than on another device
- You are logged out unexpectedly or cannot log in
- Changes you made to a website are not appearing (relevant for web developers and content editors)
- A website is running slower than usual

---

## How to clear cache and cookies

### Google Chrome

1. Open Chrome and click the **three-dot menu (⋮)** in the top-right corner.
2. Select **Settings**.
3. In the left panel, click **Privacy and security**.
4. Click **Delete browsing data**.
5. Set the **Time range** to **All time** for a complete clear.
6. Check the boxes for **Cookies and other site data** and **Cached images and files**.
7. Click **Delete data**.

### Mozilla Firefox

1. Open Firefox and click the **three-line menu (☰)** in the top-right corner.
2. Select **Settings**.
3. Click **Privacy & Security** in the left panel.
4. Scroll to the **Cookies and Site Data** section and click **Clear Data**.
5. Check both **Cookies and Site Data** and **Cached Web Content**.
6. Click **Clear**.

### Microsoft Edge

1. Open Edge and click the **three-dot menu (⋯)** in the top-right corner.
2. Select **Settings**.
3. Click **Privacy, search, and services** in the left panel.
4. Under **Clear browsing data**, click **Choose what to clear**.
5. Set the **Time range** to **All time**.
6. Check **Cookies and other site data** and **Cached images and files**.
7. Click **Clear now**.

### Safari (Mac)

1. Open Safari and click **Safari** in the menu bar.
2. Select **Settings** (or **Preferences** on older macOS versions).
3. Click the **Privacy** tab.
4. Click **Manage Website Data**, then click **Remove All**.
5. To clear the cache separately, go to **Develop → Empty Caches** in the menu bar. (If the Develop menu is not visible, go to **Settings → Advanced** and enable **Show Develop menu in menu bar**.)

---

## What happens after you clear cache and cookies

- You will be logged out of most websites and will need to sign in again.
- Saved preferences on some websites (language, theme, location) may reset.
- Pages may load slightly slower on the first visit after clearing, as the browser rebuilds the cache.
- These effects are temporary and normal.
