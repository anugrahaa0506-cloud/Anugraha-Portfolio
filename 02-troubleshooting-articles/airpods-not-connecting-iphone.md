# AirPods Not Connecting to iPhone — Troubleshooting Guide

**Product:** Apple AirPods (all generations)  
**Platform:** iPhone (iOS 14 and later)  
**Last reviewed:** April 2026  

---

## Symptom

Your AirPods do not connect automatically when removed from the case, or they appear in the Bluetooth settings as **Not Connected** despite being previously paired.

---

## Possible causes

| Cause | Likelihood |
|-------|-----------|
| AirPods connected to a different device | High |
| Bluetooth on iPhone temporarily unresponsive | High |
| AirPods battery too low to maintain connection | Medium |
| AirPods firmware needs updating | Medium |
| Corrupted pairing data | Low |

---

## Resolution steps

Work through these steps in order. Stop when the issue is resolved.

### Step 1 — Check that Bluetooth is on

1. On your iPhone, open **Settings**.
2. Tap **Bluetooth**.
3. Confirm the toggle is green (on).
4. If it was already on, toggle it off, wait 5 seconds, then toggle it back on.

### Step 2 — Check the AirPods battery

1. Place both AirPods in the charging case.
2. Open the case lid near your iPhone.
3. A battery card should appear on your iPhone screen showing charge levels.
4. If either AirPod shows below 10% battery, charge them for at least 15 minutes before continuing.

### Step 3 — Check if AirPods are connected to another device

AirPods can only actively connect to one device at a time. If they are connected to your Mac, iPad, or another iPhone, they will not auto-connect to your iPhone.

1. On any other device signed in to the same Apple ID, go to **Settings → Bluetooth**.
2. Disconnect the AirPods from that device, or turn off Bluetooth on that device temporarily.
3. On your iPhone, go to **Settings → Bluetooth** and tap your AirPods name to connect manually.

### Step 4 — Restart your iPhone

1. Press and hold the **Side button** and either **Volume button** until the power slider appears.
2. Drag the slider to turn off your iPhone.
3. Wait 30 seconds, then press and hold the **Side button** to restart.
4. Once restarted, open the AirPods case near your iPhone and check if they connect.

### Step 5 — Reset the AirPods

If none of the above steps work, reset the AirPods to factory settings and re-pair them.

1. Place both AirPods in the charging case and close the lid.
2. Wait 30 seconds, then open the lid.
3. On your iPhone, go to **Settings → Bluetooth**.
4. Tap the **Info icon (ⓘ)** next to your AirPods name.
5. Tap **Forget This Device**, then tap **Forget Device** to confirm.
6. With the case lid open, press and hold the **setup button** on the back of the case for 15 seconds. The LED will flash amber, then white.
7. Hold the open case next to your iPhone. Follow the on-screen prompts to re-pair.

---

## Expected result

After completing the applicable step, your AirPods appear as **Connected** under **My Devices** in Bluetooth settings. Audio from your iPhone routes through the AirPods automatically.

---

## If the issue persists

If your AirPods still do not connect after completing all steps above:

- Check for an iOS software update: **Settings → General → Software Update**
- Check for AirPods firmware update: AirPods update automatically when in the case and connected to a power source near a paired iPhone on Wi-Fi
- Contact Apple Support at [support.apple.com](https://support.apple.com) or visit an Apple Store for hardware diagnostics
