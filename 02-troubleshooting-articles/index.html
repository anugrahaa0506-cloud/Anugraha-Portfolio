AAirPods won't connect to iPhone

Product: Apple AirPods (all generations) · Platform: iPhone iOS 14+ · Last reviewed: April 2026

Troubleshooting

AirPods do not connect automatically
  • Open Settings on your iPhone and tap Bluetooth.
  • Confirm the Bluetooth toggle is green. If it is already on, toggle it off, wait 5 seconds, then toggle it back on.
  • Place both AirPods in the charging case and open the lid near your iPhone.
    • Checking that a battery card appears on screen — this confirms the AirPods are being detected.
    • If either AirPod shows below 10% battery, charging them for at least 15 minutes before continuing.
  • Tap your AirPods name under My Devices to connect manually if they do not connect on their own.
AirPods are connected to a different device
  • Check all devices signed in to the same Apple ID — Mac, iPad, or another iPhone.
  • Go to Settings → Bluetooth on the other device and disconnect the AirPods, or turn off Bluetooth on that device temporarily.
  • Return to your iPhone and tap your AirPods name under My Devices to connect.
AirPods appear as "Not Connected" after a restart
  • Press and hold the Side button and either Volume button until the power slider appears.
  • Drag the slider to turn off your iPhone, wait 30 seconds, then press and hold the Side button to restart.
  • Open the AirPods case near your iPhone after it restarts and check whether they connect automatically.
AirPods do not connect after trying all steps above
  • Place both AirPods in the charging case and close the lid. Wait 30 seconds, then open the lid.
  • Go to Settings → Bluetooth on your iPhone and tap the Info icon (ⓘ) next to your AirPods name.
  • Tap Forget This Device, then confirm by tapping Forget Device.
  • Press and hold the setup button on the back of the case for 15 seconds.
    • Watching for the LED to flash amber, then white — this confirms the AirPods are in pairing mode.
  • Hold the open case next to your iPhone and follow the on-screen prompts to re-pair.
💡 Tip: AirPods firmware updates automatically while charging in the case near a paired iPhone on Wi-Fi. Check for an iOS update at Settings → General → Software Update if issues persist after re-pairing.

Wi-Fi network won't show up on Windows 11

Product: Windows 11 PC / Laptop · Component: Wi-Fi adapter and network discovery · Last reviewed: April 2026

Troubleshooting

Wi-Fi is not enabled on the device
  • Click the Network icon in the taskbar (bottom-right corner).
  • Confirm the Wi-Fi tile is highlighted. If it appears greyed out, click it to enable Wi-Fi.
  • Check for a physical Wi-Fi switch on your laptop, or try the Fn + F key combination that controls the wireless adapter on your device model.
No networks appear at all
  • Confirm the router's power light is on and stable — not flashing in an error pattern.
  • Check whether at least one other device can see the network.
    • If no devices can find the network, the issue is with the router, not your PC.
  • Restart the router by unplugging it from power, waiting 30 seconds, then plugging it back in.
    • Allowing 2 minutes for the router to fully restart before checking your PC again.
Your network does not appear but others do
  • Log in to your router's admin panel — typically at 192.168.1.1 or 192.168.0.1 in a browser.
  • Confirm both 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz bands are active and broadcasting.
    • Older laptops may only support 2.4 GHz — ensure the 2.4 GHz network is enabled if your device does not support 5 GHz.
  • Check whether the network name (SSID) is set to hidden. If it is, connect manually:
    • Clicking the Network icon in the taskbar, then selecting Manage Wi-Fi connections.
    • Scrolling to the bottom and selecting Hidden network.
    • Entering the network name (SSID) and password exactly as configured on your router.
Network is visible but connection fails repeatedly
  • Open Settings (Windows key + I) and go to System → Troubleshoot → Other troubleshooters.
  • Click Run next to Internet Connections and follow the on-screen instructions.
  • Right-click the Start button, select Device Manager, and expand Network adapters.
  • Right-click your Wi-Fi adapter and select Update driver.
    • Choosing Search automatically for drivers and following the prompts.
    • If no update is found, visiting your laptop manufacturer's support site to download the latest Wi-Fi driver for your model.
Network still does not appear after all steps above
  • Open Settings → Network and Internet → Advanced network settings and click Network reset.
  • Click Reset now and confirm. Your PC will restart automatically.
  • Reconnect to your Wi-Fi network using your password after the restart.
⚠️ Important: Network reset removes all saved Wi-Fi passwords and network configurations. Re-enter credentials for all networks after completing this step.
💡 Tip: For corporate or enterprise networks, contact your IT administrator — the network may have MAC address filtering or access restrictions that prevent your device from connecting.
